A/B testing virtual try-on on Shopify product pages

Compare product page behavior with and without try-on. Why built-in A/B buckets beat guessing conversion impact.

May 10, 2026

Many merchants launch try-on and ask: did it work? Without a control group, you cannot know whether cart lifts came from try-on or from seasonality, ads, or pricing changes.

What Stylab A/B testing does

From the Widget admin page, enable A/B testing and choose what share of visitors see try-on (10% to 90%). Each visitor is bucketed consistently. The control group sees the normal product page; the test group sees the try-on button.

After 30 days, compare for each bucket:

  • Page views
  • Try-on sessions (test group)
  • Add-to-cart count and rate

How to run a clean test

  1. Pick 2–5 products with steady traffic (hoodies, jerseys, bestsellers)
  2. Start at 50/50 split for 30 days
  3. Do not change pricing or hero images mid-test
  4. Review results before enabling try-on store-wide

What A/B testing does not promise

A/B results vary by traffic, product type, photos, and store experience. Stylab helps you measure — it does not guarantee a specific conversion lift.
